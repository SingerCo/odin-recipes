Coconut Curry Red Lentil Dahl with Tomatoes, Cilantro & Rice

Description

It's almost impossible to mess up this lentil dahl. A perfect dish for leftovers, a delight to eat piping hot or pulled out from the fridge. Excellent sweetness, creamy textures. Don't forget to go heavier on the spices, garlic, and ginger!

Ingredients - 4 servings

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 32 fl oz chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 small bunch cilantro
  • 1 (13.5 fl oz) can coconut milk
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 2 (1 inch) pieces ginger root
  • 1 pint grape tomatoes
  • 2 jalapeño peppers
  • 2 medium yellow onions
  • 1 cup red lentils, dried
  • cinnamon, ground
  • cumin, ground
  • curry powder
  • extra virgin olive oil
  • salt
  • turmeric, ground

Steps

  1. Using a strainer or colander, rinse the rice under cold, running water, then drain and transfer to a medium saucepan. Add broth and bring the mixture to a boil over high heat.
  2. Meanwhile, wash and dry the fresh produce.
  3. Once the liquid comes to a boil, stir the mixture, cover the saucepan, and reduce heat to low. Cook rice until liquid is full absorbed, 15-18 minutes. Once done, remove rice from the heat and let it stand, still covered, for 5 minutes.
  4. While the rice is cooking, peel and mince ginger and garlic; place in a small bowl.
  5. Quarter jalapeño peppers lengthwise; seed and remove ribs with a spoon. Mince and add to the bowl with the ginger and the garlic.
  6. Peel and small dice onions.
  7. Preheat another medium saucepan over medium heat.
  8. Once the pan is hot, add oil and swirl to coat the bottom. Add ginger, garlic, jalapeño, onion, and spices to the pan. Cook, stirring occasionally, until onion is soft, 8-12 minutes.
  9. Meanwhile, rinse the lentils in a colander or strainer.
  10. Add lentils, broth, and coconut milk to the pan. Bring the mixture to a boil over high heat; once boiling, reduce to a simmer, cover, and cook until the lentils are just tender, about 20-40 minutes.
  11. While the lentils are cooking, quarter tomatoes.
  12. Shave cilantro leaves off the stems; discard stems and mince the leaves.
  13. When the lentils are done, add tomatoes and half the cilantro (reserving the rest for garnish) to the pan; stir to combine and continue to cook until heated through, 2-3 minutes more.
  14. To serve, divide lentils and rice between bowls; top with reserved cilantro and enjoy!
