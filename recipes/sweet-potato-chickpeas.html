Mediterranean Sweet Potatoes with Chickpeas & Garlic-Yogurt Sauce

Description

Looking for a simple, easy meal, that's absolutely delightful, full of flavor, and never gets old? Look no further. This is one of my favorite meals. It was also how I even started with cooking. I will still make it every couple months because it's such a fantastic melding of flavors.

This recipe mixes the sweet dryness of sweet potatoes with the savory crunch of roasted chickpeas. The tomatoes add a breath of acidity while the garlic-yogurt sauce adds a liquidity that freshens up the dish and prevents it from being too dry. It helped foster a love of cooking for me, and started me on my own cooking journey. I'll forever be grateful to this meal, and I'll never stop eating it! Highly recommend you give it a shot as well.

Ingredients - 4 servings

  • 2 (15 oz) cans of garbanzo beans
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 pints grape tomatoes
  • 1 small bunch Italian (flat-leaf) parsley
  • 2 lemons
  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 2lb sweet potatoes
  • black pepper
  • cinnamon, ground
  • coriander, ground
  • cumin, ground
  • dill, dried
  • extra virgin olive oil
  • paprika
  • salt

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 450 degrees Fahrenheit. Line a large sheet pan with aluminum foil.
  2. Wash and dry the fresh produce.
  3. Peel and cut sweet potatoes lengthwise into 4-6 wedges each, depending on size, then transfer to the sheet pan. Drizzle with oil and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Spread wedges out in an even layer, then place in oven and bake for 10 minutes, until potatoes begin to soften slightly.
  5. Meanwhile, drain and rinse the beans in a colander; set aside to drain further.
  6. Juice on lemon into a medium bowl.
  7. Place drained chickpeas in the bowl with the lemon juice; add oil, cumin, coriander, paprika, cinnamon, and salt. Toss to coat.
  8. Remove sweet potatoes from the oven and flip using tongs. Sprinkle chickpeas (and any leftover seasoning) over the sweet potatoes in an even layer and return to the oven. Bake until chickpeas become crispy and potatoes can be pierced easily with a fork, 20-25 minutes.
  9. Meanwhile, grate the zest from the remaining lemon into a small bowl, then juice into the same bowl.
  10. Peal and mince garlic; add to the bowl with the lemon zest and juice.
  11. Add yogurt, dill, salt, and pepper to the bowl and whisk to combine the sauce. Add 2-3 tablespoons cold water, if needed, to reach drizzling consistency.
  12. Halve the grape tomatoes crosswise.
  13. Shave parsley leaves off the stems; discard the stems and mince the leaves.
  14. To server, divide potatoes and chickpeas between plates. Top with tomatoes, parsley, and garlic-yogurt sauce. Enjoy!
