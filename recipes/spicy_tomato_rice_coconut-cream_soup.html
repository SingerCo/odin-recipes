Spicy Tomato-Rice Coconut Cream Soup with Zucchini, Beans & Cilantro

Description

Who doesn't love a nice spicy warm soup on a cold winter's day? If you're looking for one, this delicious tomato-rice soup is a perfect place to start. A stupendous backing base of onion, chili-garlic sauce, and some garlic and ginger is blended with rice and zucchini to create a rich, creamy soup that will tingle your toes and your tongue.

It's also a great way to pick up spicing a dish. When I was learning to cook, one thing I appreciated about this meal is how adaptable it was. You get the chance to adjust spices and add a dollop of what you think it could use a bit more of, but in two places! Right before you puree the soup, and at the very end of cooking. I like to add some cayenne for some extra spice, but some cumin, perhaps some basil would be a nice touch too.

Ingredients - 4 servings

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 2 (15 oz) cans cannellini beans
  • 32 fl oz chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 small bunch cilantro
  • 1 (13.5 fl oz) can coconut milk
  • 2 (14.5 oz) cans diced tomatoes
  • 4 cloves garlic (double if you have taste)
  • 2 (1 inch) pieces ginger root
  • 1 medium yellow onion
  • 2 medium zucchini squash
  • chili-garlic sauce
  • salt
  • virgin coconut oil

Steps

  1. Wash and dry the fresh produce.
  2. Peel and mince garlic and ginger.
  3. Preheat a large pot over medium heat.
  4. While the pot heats up, peel and small dice onion.
  5. Once the pot is hot, add oil and swirl to coat the bottom. Add garlic, ginger, onion, 2 tsp chili-garlic sauce, and salt; cook, stirring occasionally, until onion begins to soften, 6-8 minutes.
  6. Add broth, tomatoes, and coconut milk to the pot. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce heat to low and simmer, stirring occasionally, for 5 minutes.
  7. Meanwhile, trim and halve zucchini lengthwise, then slice crosswise into half rounds.
  8. Drain and rinse beans.
  9. Puree the soup with an immersion blender or regular blender. Return pureed soup to the pot and stir in zucchini, beans, and rice.
  10. Bring soup to a boil over high heat, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer, stirring occasionally, until rice is tender, about 15-18 minutes.
  11. Meanwhile, shave cilantro leaves off the stems; discard stems and mince the leaves.
  12. To serve, divide soup between bowls and top with cilantro. Enjoy!
